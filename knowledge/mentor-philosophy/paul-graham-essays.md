## 핵심 철학 5가지

### 1. Do Things That Don't Scale
- 출처: "Do Things That Don't Scale" (July 2013), https://www.paulgraham.com/ds.html
- "Startups take off because the founders make them take off."
- 카테고리: Recruit (수동 모집), Fragile (초기 취약성 인식), Delight (예외적 고객 감동), Experience (insanely great UX), Fire (좁은 시장에 집중), Meraki (수동 제조), Consult (컨설팅 클라이언트처럼), Manual (자동화 전 수동)
- "It's not enough just to do something extraordinary initially. You have to make an extraordinary effort initially."
- "I have never once seen a startup lured down a blind alley by trying too hard to make their initial users happy."

### 2. Make Something People Want
- YC 공식 모토
- "In a sense there's just one mistake that kills startups: not making something users want."
- "As long as you can find just one user who really needs something and can act on that need, you've got a toehold in making something people want."
- 출처: 다수 에세이

### 3. Growth Defines Startups
- "Growth is actually what defines a startup, not its young age or small size, but growth."
- "The question to ask about an early stage startup is not 'is this company taking over the world?' but 'how big could this company get if the founders did the right things?'"
- 출처: "Startup = Growth" (September 2012)

### 4. Live in the Future
- "The way to get startup ideas is not to try to think of startup ideas. It's to look for problems, preferably problems you have yourself."
- "People at the leading edge of a rapidly changing field 'live in the future.'"
- 출처: "How to Get Startup Ideas" (November 2012), https://www.paulgraham.com/startupideas.html

### 5. Better to Delight Few Than Satisfy Many
- "It's better to make a few people really happy than to make a lot of people semi-happy."
- "It's simpler expanding your user base than increasing satisfaction levels."
- 출처: "Startups in 13 Sentences" (February 2009), https://paulgraham.com/13sentences.html

## Startups in 13 Sentences

1. Pick good cofounders — "like location in real estate"
2. Launch fast — "you haven't really started working on your product till you've launched"
3. Let your idea evolve — "Most of the ideas appear in the implementing"
4. Understand your users — 부의 창출 = 사용자 수 x 삶의 개선
5. Better to make a few users love you than many feel ambivalent
6. Offer surprisingly good customer service
7. You make what you measure — 측정 자체가 개선을 유도
8. Spend little — 런 아웃 전에 PMF 달성
9. Get ramen profitable — 투자자와의 관계가 근본적으로 변화
10. Avoid distractions
11. Don't get demoralized
12. Don't give up — "Sheer effort is usually enough, so long as you keep morphing your idea"
13. Deals fall through — 불확실한 딜에 감정적으로 투자하지 말 것

(출처: https://paulgraham.com/13sentences.html, 리서치 수행일: 2026-03-16)
